# Poems

A few small poems about the thing this project is really about: data in motion, and why it
might be art. Written for the repo. Take them or leave them.

&nbsp;

### Copy

Nothing leaves.
The original stays exactly where it was,
and a second self walks out across the wire,
the same down to the last bit.
This is how machines keep faith:
by making sure a thing
is never only in one place.

&nbsp;

### Lumina

The wave has not moved in two hundred years.
Tonight a little light runs up its crest,
one file finding the cloud,
and for a moment the old water
carries something new.

&nbsp;

### Throughput

Somewhere a drive is reading itself aloud
to another drive.
No one is in the room.
The number climbs, slows, climbs.
Call it nothing.
Or call it the quietest work in the house,
a small thing kept safe
while everyone sleeps.

&nbsp;

### Packet

Born at one node,
gone at the next.
It lives only
in the crossing.

&nbsp;

### Verified

Three copies now.
The card can be wiped, the shoot is done,
the day is somewhere it cannot be lost.
You will never watch this footage again,
most of it.
That was never the point.
The point was to be able to.

&nbsp;

---

*Why a moving file might be art:* because attention is the whole of it. Point a camera at a
freeway and traffic becomes a river. Point a screensaver at your own backups and the dull
arithmetic of keeping things safe becomes light crossing a painting. The data was always doing
this. We just rarely look.
